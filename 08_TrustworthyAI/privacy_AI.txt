### higher delta 
In this scenario, while each individual query or data extraction from the data is heavily protected (low ε), the overall system has a higher probability (higher δ) of failing to maintain its privacy guarantees at some point. This could mean that, although the system is very private on a per-query basis, there's a non-negligible chance that privacy could be compromised due to the higher delta.

Privacy-Utility Trade-off: A lower epsilon generally means more noise is added to the data or model, leading to potentially lower utility or accuracy. However, it provides stronger privacy for each operation.
